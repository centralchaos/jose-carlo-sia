## Mission

I have worked with data analytics, statistical computing, and data-driven systems since 2011, when I began using Python to analyze national-scale sales and operations data at Del Monte Philippines. That analytical work developed alongside a career in enterprise systems, infrastructure, and technical delivery. I have since applied both to agriculture, animal health, and genomics, where dependable data systems support food security, biosecurity, and access to science.

Data is the constant. For more than a decade my work has been to turn noisy, real-world signals into decisions, whether the output is a national sales dashboard, live farm telemetry, or a genomic surveillance pipeline.

Advanced science is hard to operate under real Philippine field conditions: intermittent power, limited connectivity, small teams, and tight budgets. I design for those constraints. The result is a set of systems that can run locally, remain auditable, and continue operating well beyond the pilot stage.

## What I build

- **Field systems.** Tunnel-ventilated poultry climate control, swine environmental monitoring, RFID and telemetry on ESP32-class hardware.
- **Laboratory and genomics infrastructure.** Galaxy environments, containerized pipelines, LIMS, and the metadata and provenance standards that keep genetic data traceable.
- **Data analysis and decision support.** More than a decade of Python-based data analysis and data science: cleaning and modeling telemetry, feature engineering, anomaly detection, and models for disease classification and animal vocalization analysis, applied where the data justifies it.

I approach genomics primarily as a systems architect. My contribution is disciplined, enterprise-grade engineering that complements the work of biologists, veterinarians, laboratory scientists, agriculturists, and bioinformaticians.

---

## Engineering principles

- Design for intermittent power, constrained connectivity, small teams, and limited budgets.
- Keep data traceable from device or specimen through analysis to report.
- Use AI only when it creates measurable operational or analytical value.
- Prefer local-first, auditable, and maintainable systems over fragile demonstrations.
- Build with domain experts so the technology respects biology, operations, and regulation.

## Publication

[![DOI](https://img.shields.io/badge/DOI-10.5281%2Fzenodo.21308607-blue)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21308607)

**[GEMS: A Genomic Evidence Management Framework for Regulatory Decision-Making](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21308607)**  
Jose Carlo Sia · KE-Texh · 2026 · CC BY 4.0

The paper defines an eleven-stage evidence lifecycle, six evidentiary pillars, and the Genomic Evidence Standard (GES). It demonstrates the framework through a portable food-species genomics implementation using Oxford Nanopore sequencing, LIMS integration, evidence scoring, integrity hashing, and structured reporting.
